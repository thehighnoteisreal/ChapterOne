Two Kinds of Messages

Posted: February 24

There have been hundreds of accounts of a message which was delivered via MySpace, Friendster, Twitter, and also, of course, Facebook. The method for deliver seems to have changed as the Internet evolved, but the purpose of the messages remain the same.

During my research, I’ve discovered that there are actually two types of messages. There are the ones like the email to Jill which asked for her to list her problems, and then there is a second type which seems to make an attempt to connect the recipient with someone else in need. This other person is typically a stranger.

During the last ten years, there are several communities which have been started up around these messages. Recipients describe their reaction to the message as “confused and a little scared.” But, there is also an element of curiosity. How legitimate are these mysterious messages?

I wasn’t able to piece all of this together until I happened across a community called The High Notes Club. This was a group of people who believed that the messages were being sent as a part of a higher purpose, and that it was the responsibility of the recipient to follow through. At first, this sounds like the mentality of a chain letter, but beneath the surface it represents so much more.
